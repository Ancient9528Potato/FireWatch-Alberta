MMA 616 · Group Project

FireWatch Alberta — Living Spec

A pre-season benchmarking dashboard for Alberta Wildfire. Compares each of the 10 forest areas against its own 20-year history — by fire count, hectares burned, and cause mix — so a planning analyst can recommend where to concentrate crews and prevention effort before peak season.

"This lets an Alberta wildfire planning analyst see which forest areas are running above their 20-year norm, and whether the cause is preventable, before the season peaks — so they can place crews and prevention effort with a defensible data trail."

Overview

The problem it solves

Before each fire season (planning window runs through April, ahead of the May–Aug peak), Alberta Wildfire must decide where to pre-position Initial Attack crews and air tankers, and where to concentrate prevention effort. Today that means hand-assembling 20 years of fire records across 10 forest areas and ~15 cause categories — roughly a half-day of spreadsheet work that hides the signal.

The leverage: this turns that half-day into a 5-minute read — which forest areas run hottest relative to their own 20-year norm, whether the load is lightning (→ detection/suppression) or human (→ prevention), with the historical trail behind every number.

User & Decision

Primary user

A risk / emergency-planning analyst at Alberta Wildfire Management Branch. Has fire-domain expertise; is short on time and tooling. Produces a recommendation to the Director of Operations — does not set policy alone.

The two decisions this changes

Lever 1
Where to pre-position Initial Attack crews and air tankers before the season. Resources placed in a low-risk area are wasted; under-covering a hot area lets a fire escape where suppression effort multiplies.
Lever 2
Where to concentrate prevention — fire bans, public education, industry restrictions. The cause mix (lightning vs. human) is the key determinant: human-caused fires are preventable; lightning-caused fires are not.

Success Criteria

Each standard is scoreable — a reviewer can check whether the dashboard passes or fails.

  • Every percentage carries its N. "High Level is 14% of fires" must render as "14.3% (3,970 / 27,828)".
  • Fire size is reported as median + 90th percentile + total hectares, never a bare mean. (Verified: mean 262 ha vs median 0.02 ha — a bare mean is disqualifying.)
  • SIZE_CLASS (A–E) is shown as a labelled category, never averaged.
  • Any cause trend that crosses 2012 carries the reclassification note (Agriculture/Government split out of Resident/Other Industry that year).
  • Cause breakdowns using ACTIVITY_CLASS / TRUE_CAUSE display the null share (~61% / ~53% filled).
  • Every view states the FPA-only scope (Forest Protection Area — excludes "Mutual Aid" fires outside it) where a total could be mistaken for all of Alberta.
  • The Signal panel shows the month distribution of fires within the 3-year flagging window so the analyst can judge whether elevated count falls inside the May–Aug peak.
  • All charts use a colorblind-safe palette (Okabe-Ito). No red/green encoding.

Gold example — the centrepiece answer the dashboard must reproduce exactly

High Level forest area — 20-year benchmark 3,970 fires 2006–2025 (14.3% of the FPA total of 27,828). Cause mix: Lightning 47.0% (1,865), Resident 18.9% (752), Incendiary 17.6% (698), Agriculture 4.8% (189), other 11.7% (N = 3,970). Median fire size 0.20 ha; 90th percentile 17.7 ha; total 2.33M ha — among the highest in the province, concentrated in escape years (2023, 2019, 2011). Read: lightning and human causes are nearly even here, so both detection reach AND prevention matter — unlike a pure-lightning northern area. (FPA-only; cost and forecast out of scope.)

Scope

Smallest valuable version (ships Day 2)

🚨 Signal panel (landing screen)

Watch list for each of the 10 forest areas. An area is flagged when its recent-3-year mean annual fire count or hectares is ≥ 1.3× its 2006–2025 median annual. Count and hectares are flagged independently. The 1.3× threshold is analyst-adjustable — it is an assumed starting value, not a derived standard.

📊 Region benchmark card

Selectable per area. Shows: fire count + % of FPA total (with N), total hectares, median + p90 fire size, cause mix as % with counts, and a plain-language "Read" paragraph. Click any stat for a data trail.

🔁 Cause-mix panel

~15 GENERAL_CAUSE categories with explicit Human-vs-Lightning grouping (see Business Rules), per region and year. Null share always shown.

📈 Annual trend

Fire count and total hectares 2006–2025 for selected region and all-FPA. 2012 reclassification break marked on the axis.

Stretch goal

⏱ Response-time view

Median lag from REPORTED_DATE → FIRE_FIGHTING_START_DATE (this exact pair; not dispatch→arrival), on the ~71% of fires with the second field. The 29% gap is disclosed — not assumed random.

Human-vs-Lightning definition (explicit)

Required grouping — never infer from context Lightning: GENERAL_CAUSE = 'Lightning' only.
Human-caused: Recreation · Resident · Incendiary · Agriculture Industry · Forest Industry · Oil & Gas Industry · Other Industry · Power Line Industry · Government · Railroad.
Indeterminate: Undetermined · Under Investigation · Restart · Prescribed Fire — shown separately, never collapsed into either group.

Business Rules

These rules are domain/statistical constraints. They apply regardless of how the agent is prompted and cannot be overridden without an audit log entry.

Norm baseline
The norm = 20-yr (2006–2025) mean annual value, computed separately for fire count and total hectares. Use mean, not median — annual hectares is so skewed that a median-based norm produces ratios in the hundreds (verified: 361×, 337×).
Watch-list flag
An area is flagged when its recent-3-years (2023–2025) mean annual value is ≥ 1.3× its 20-yr mean. Count and hectares are flagged independently. 1.3× is the analyst-adjustable threshold.
Size reporting
Always median + p90 + total hectares together. Never a bare mean. Never average SIZE_CLASS.
Cross-2012 cause trends
Always carry the reclassification note: Agriculture Industry and Government were split out of Resident and Other Industry in 2012.
Cause-detail views
ACTIVITY_CLASS / TRUE_CAUSE views always display the null share (60.6% / 52.5% filled province-wide).
FPA scope
State "FPA only" wherever a total could be read as all-Alberta. Mutual Aid fires outside the Forest Protection Area are excluded.

Must Never Produce

These are hard prohibitions. Violating any one disqualifies a view.
  • Causal language ("causes" / "drives") for a statistical association — use "associated with."
  • A bare mean fire size, or any use of the word "average" applied to CURRENT_SIZE without showing median and p90 alongside it.
  • An averaged SIZE_CLASS — it is an ordinal category, not a number.
  • A dollar or cost figure — no cost field exists in this data.
  • A forecast ("next season will…") — this is a finalized historical record.
  • A climate-attribution claim — no FWI or climate series is present.
  • An FPA total presented as all-Alberta.
  • A "human-caused" grouping without explicitly naming which GENERAL_CAUSE values are included.
  • A position on the full-suppression-vs-managed-fire policy debate.

Human-in-the-Loop

Human has final word on

  • The deployment / prevention recommendation
  • Publishing or sharing any view
  • Whether a region is comparable across the 2012 cause reclassification

Override log — not optional

Any threshold change or flag override is logged with: who changed it · original flag/value · why. No log, no change. Log is stored in localStorage key firewatchOverrides and appended to any export.

Schema: { timestamp, analystId, panel, originalValue, newValue, reason }

Data Profile

Source

Alberta Forestry & Parks — Historical Wildfire Database 2006–2025.
Open Government Licence – Alberta.
Dictionary dated March 19, 2026.

One row

One wildfire incident inside the Forest Protection Area (FPA), from ignition to extinguishment. 50 fields covering identity, location, size, cause chain, response timing, fire status progression, and weather/fuel at initial assessment.

Scale

27,828 fire records, 2006–2025 (20 complete years).
Annual range: 723 (2020) – 1,954 (2006). Typical year ~1,200–1,900.
2025 is a complete year (1,277 fires).

Coverage

FPA only. Fires outside the Forest Protection Area ("Mutual Aid" fires) are excluded. Any provincial total understates total Alberta fire activity.
This is the full administrative record within the FPA — not a sample.

Seasonality (verified from FIRE_START_DATE)

Peak season: May–August. May is the single largest month (6,423 fires), then July (5,693), June (4,643), August (3,273). Off-season (November–February) is small but non-zero.

Data Cautions

Each line in knowledge/data-cautions.md passes the Deletion Test: remove it and a wrong number ships.

Cause categories changed mid-series — flag every cross-2012 trend
  • Agriculture Industry introduced 2012; pre-2012 fires sit under Resident
  • Government introduced 2012; pre-2012 under Other Industry
  • Prescribed Fire used only 2012–2016 (19 records total)
  • Restart used only until 2012
A "Resident fires over time" line will show a false drop in 2012 purely from reclassification — always note the break.
Detection/response timing fields are partial REPORTED_DATE 100% · DISPATCH_DATE 99.9% · FIRE_FIGHTING_START_DATE 71.2% · IA_ARRIVAL_AT_FIRE_DATE 70.8%. Response-time analysis must report on the ~71% with data and disclose the missing 29% — do not assume the gap is random.
Weather fields are point-in-time at initial assessment, not the weather that drove the fire's full run Do not treat TEMPERATURE / RELATIVE_HUMIDITY / WIND_SPEED as season or daily climate.

Domain Notes

From knowledge/domain-research.md — treat as claims to verify against the data, not established facts.

Patterns to verify

  • Lightning is the single largest cause by count (~35%) but human causes dominate in aggregate — verify per region, since the split drives whether strategy is prevention vs. detection.
  • Top 1% of fires ≈ 97% of all hectares burned — verify. If true, "fire count" and "hectares burned" tell different stories.
  • 2023 and 2024 were severe years in Alberta — verify against CURRENT_SIZE totals before stating it.

Contested — stay neutral

Whether aggressive full-suppression raises long-run risk by building fuel load is debated among fire scientists. The dashboard shows the historical pattern and must not take a side on suppression policy.
