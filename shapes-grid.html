FireWatch Alberta — four shapes of the deliverable

Same chosen concept (pre-season regional benchmark). Four distinct forms, compared, then converged. Schematic — content is placeholder.

A · Region card grid

10 forest-area cards; click one to expand its benchmark.
+ All 10 areas visible at once; good overview.
– No single focal answer; the gold example competes with 9 peers for the eye.
Converged

B · Watch-list + focus card

Signal list on top; one big benchmark card below as the centrepiece.
+ One unmistakable focal point — the gold example owns the screen.
+ Signal panel answers "where do I look first?" immediately.
– Shows one region's detail at a time (acceptable: the decision is one area at a time).

C · Map-led drill-down

Alberta map with the 10 areas; click a region to open its panel.
+ Geography is intuitive; spatial pattern reads instantly.
– Needs a GIS layer we have not connected; lat/long is per-fire, not per-area boundary. Over-budget for week one.

D · Ranked leaderboard

Sortable table: all 10 areas ranked by count / hectares / ratio-to-norm.
+ Dense, sortable, great for ranking; honest about count-vs-hectares split.
– A table is a tool, not an answer; no gold example, weak for a 2-min show-don't-tell.
